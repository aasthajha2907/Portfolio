The Origin
On the record

She made history class
worth reading.

Long before she was building brands, she was building audiences — starting with her 8th standard class and a comic book nobody asked for.

In 8th standard, I turned an entire history syllabus into a comic book because textbooks bored me. My class used it to study. Highest average that semester. I was thirteen — and I'd accidentally figured out the thing that would define my career: understand your audience deeply enough and you can make anything compelling.

"Understand your audience deeply enough and you can make anything compelling."

Before I write a word, I sit with the people who built the product. Engineers, founders, product managers. I want every feature, every edge case, every detail that didn't make the pitch deck. Because what makes customers love a product is rarely the headline USP. It's quieter. More specific. Usually in a Notion doc nobody showed the marketing team.

My job is to find it. Name it. Build a brand around it that sticks.

I wear the brand's tone — I don't impose my own. Every project gets obsessive research and the kind of prep that makes winging it impossible. I work best with brands that have a reason to exist beyond the bottom line.

Off the clock: chronically online, overwatering my plants, on my third coffee. Introvert. Occasionally found talking to a pothos.

DECISION FATIGUE
Hypothetical campaign concept — Swiggy

Swiggy isn't
a food app.
It's a decision
outsourcing engine.

Nobody is advertising the 7 minutes before the order. That's the gap.

The real insight

Everyone in this category is screaming fast delivery, hot food, best price. Meanwhile the actual user just stared at their fridge for 7 minutes and closed it. People don't open Swiggy because they're hungry — they open it because they're burnt out, had a bad day, need a win. Market the mood. Not the meal.


Zomato is loud, witty, the brand that talks at you. Swiggy could own something quieter — the app that feels like it was made for your worst Tuesdays. That's a position nobody can take once you own it.

Headline options

— For when deciding is harder than eating.
02 You don't need a plan. Just open Swiggy.
03 We already knew. You just had to open the app.
04 Brain offline. Swiggy online.
05 You kept it together all day. The evening owes you.
01
The Fridge Moment
Advertise the 7 minutes before the order. The scroll, the indecision, the "ugh fine." That's the real trigger and nobody's touching it.
02
Mood not Hunger
Tired. Bad day. Need a win. Main character night. Every emotional state maps to a meal — and Swiggy already knows which one.
03
You Survived Today
Not "treat yourself" — that's tired. More like: "You kept it together all day. The least the evening can do is not involve cooking."
04
It Already Knows
Swiggy knows your comfort food, your 11pm patterns, your bad decisions. Lean into it — not creepy, just accurate.
